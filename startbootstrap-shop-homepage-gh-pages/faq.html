Frequently Asked Questions

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience while trying on bridal dresses, we recommend wearing something comfortable and easy to change in and out of, such as a simple dress or a button-up top with leggings. This will make the fitting process quicker and more convenient.

For undergarments, we suggest bringing a white or nude bra and panties, as these will provide the best foundation for more dresses, ensuring a seamless and flattering fit. If you plan on wearing shapewear or a specific corset under your gown, feel free to bring those along as well, as they can affect the fit and overall look of your dress.

Shoes are another important consideration. If you already have the heels or flats you plan to wear on your wedding day, bring them with you. If not, we recommend bringing a pair with a similar heel height so you can get an accurate sense of how your dress will fall and move.

Trying on wedding gowns is an exciting and special experience, but it can also be different from trying on regular dresses due to the intricate designs and delicate fabrics. Here's everything you need to know about getting changed into your bridal gown during your appointment.

What should I wear under the gown?

To maintain hygiene and ensure your comfort, we kindly ask that all brides wear panties at all times while trying on gowns. While some wedding dresses come with built-in support and do not require a bra, for sanitary reasons, you will also be required to wear a bra while trying on dresses. If you are unsure whether your gown should be worn with a bra on your wedding day, your consultant can guide you based on the gown’s design and fit.

✔ Nude or seamless underwear is recommended to prevent visible lines under the gowns.
✔ A strapless bra can be helpful for certain dress styles but is not always necessary.
✔ Shapewear, garters, or stockings are completely optional—you are welcome to wear them if you plan to on your wedding day!

Should I wear shoes?

Shoes are not required while trying on gowns in the salon, but we highly recommend bringing a pair of heels with a similar height to your wedding shoes. This helpss you visualize how the gown will fall and allows us to make any necessary length adjustments during fittings. If you already have your wedding shoes, feel free to bring them along!

Getting into the gown

Wedding gowns are often made with heavy fabrics, intricate lace, long trains, and multiple layers, which can make them more challenging to put on than regular clothing. Many gowns also have delicate fastenings, corset backs, or dozens of buttons, which require assistance to secure properly.

If you are comfortable, your bridal consultant will be happy to assist you in getting into your gown. Our consultants are highly trained professionals, so there's no need to be embarrassed - they are there to ensure the gown fits you properly and to make the process as seamless as possible. Alternatively, if you prefer, one of your guests can help you change. We want you to feel completely at ease, so just let us know your preference!

How long does it take to get dressed?

Due to the complexity of wedding gowns, expect it to take a few minutes to get into each gown. Some gowns have multiple layers, intricate buttons, or corset lacing that require careful fastening. This is perfectly normal, and your consultant will guide you through the process to ensure everything looks perfect.

If you have any questions or special requests, don't hesitate to ask - we want to make your experience comfortable, enjoyable and truly magical! 💕👰✨

First of all, congratulations on finding your dream wedding gown! This is an exciting milestone, and we're here to guide you through the next steps to ensure a smooth and stress-free experience.

1. Securing your Gown

To reserve your gown, we require at 25% deposit of the toal purchase price. This deposit must be paid immediately before you leave the salon to officially secure your dress. The deposit will be credit toward your total balance, with the remaining amount due at a later stage.

2. Scheduling your Tailoring & Alterations Appointments

Once your gown is reserved, we will book you in for a tailoring and alterations consultation. Since most wedding gowns require adjustments for the perfect fit, we will work with you to schedule your neccessary fittings. The number of alteration appointments varies depending on the gown and the adjustments required.

3. Final Fitting & Payment Completion

After your final fitting, once you are completely satisfied with your gown's fit and design, the remaining balance must be paid in full before you can take you dress home. At this point, we will carefully steam and prepare your gown for pickup, ensuring it is in pristine condition for your big day.

4. Payment Methods

We accept the following payment methods:
  • All major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)
  • E-transfer sent directly to the salon
Please note we do not accept cash, cheques, cryptocurrency, or other alternative payment methods.

5. Bringing your Gown Home

Once your final payment is made, your gown is offically yours! We recommend bringing someone with you to help carry and transport the dress safely. We also provide storage and care tips to keep your gown in perfect condition until your wedding day.

At Caitlin's Bridal, we want to provide every bride with an excceptional and personalised shopping experience. Whether you're searching for your dream wedding gown or looking for the perfect accessories, here's what you need to know about appointments.

Bridal Gown Appointments (Required)

If you're planning to try on bridal gowns, an appointment is required to ensure you receive the dedicated time and attention you deserve. Booking an appointment reserves a private fitting room and an expert bridal consultant you will guide you through our collection, help you explore different styles, and assist you in finding the gown that best fits your vision, body type, and budget.

Appointments allow us to create a stress-free and memorable experience, ensuring you have the time and space to make this important decision without feeling rushed. We highly recommend booking in advance, espically during weekends and peak wedding seasons, as appointment slots fill up quickly.

Accessories & Lingerie Shopping (No Appointment Required)

If you're visiting us to browse or purchase shoes, veils, lingerie, jewellery, or other bridal accessories, an appointment is not required. You're welcome to stop by anytime during our business hours to explore our selection at your convenience. Our stylist will be available to assist you, answer any questions, and help you find the perfect finishing touches for your bridal look.

Try to schedule your first bridal appointment at least a year before your wedding. This gives you time to explore different styles, revist dresses if needed, and make a confident decision.

Final Fittings & Alterations

Once your dress arrives, alterations typically require two to three fittings over a period of six to eight weeks Be sure to factor in this time so that your gown fits flawlessly by your big day.

Starting early ensures a stress-free experience and gives you the best chance of securing your dream dress.

Bridal appointments are typically scheduled for two hours, giving you plenty of time to try on a variety of gowns, experiment with different silhouettes, and get a feel for what you love most. If you haven't found "the one" by the end of your appointment, don't worry - it's completely normal! Many brides need time to reflect on their options or try on additional dresses before making a final decision. If you need more time, we'll happily schedule a follow-up appointment as soon as possible, ensuring you work with the same bridal consultant for consistency.

Can I Shop for Accessories During my Bridal Appointment?

Absolutely! While your primary focus will be on finding your wedding dress, you are more than welcome to explore accessories such as:

  • Veils - Whether you're considering a dramatic cathedral-length veil or a chic birdcage style, trying them on with your dress can help you envision the complete look.
  • Shoes - Finding the right pair is crucial, espically for heel height and comfort. if you already have a specific pair in mind, bring them along!
  • Lingerie & Shapewear - Your consultant can offer guidance on the best undergarments to complement your gown's style and fit.
  • Jewellery & Hairpieces - Statement earrings, delicate tiaras, or vintage-inspired hair combs can all elevate your bridal look.

If there is time left in your appointment, your bridal consultant will glady assist you in selecting accessories. However, if you need additional time or a more in-depth styling session, we can schedule a seperate accessory appointment or have one of our specialists from another department assist you.

By planning ahead and exploring all your options, you'll leave feeling confident and exicted about your bridal ensemble from head to toe! ✨👰
